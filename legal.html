Legal Architecture

Flat fees. Your NPI. No referral money.

SurgeonValue is software licensed to surgical practices at a flat, fair-market price. It never pays for or takes a cut of referrals, downstream revenue, or payer contracts. This page is the accounting of how the product is structured to stay AKS and Stark compliant by design.

Last updated April 17, 2026. Nothing here is legal advice.

hub
1 · What SurgeonValue is

Software platform. Not an MSO. Not a provider.

Clear

SurgeonValue is a software platform (SaaS) licensed to orthopedic surgical practices. It is not a Management Services Organization, it is not a billing company, and it does not deliver clinical care. The surgeon owns the patient relationship, the panel, the clinical decisions, and the billing entity.

Revenue captured through the platform flows directly to the surgeon's own billing entity under the surgeon's own NPI. SurgeonValue never bills a payer on the surgeon's behalf and never holds funds that belong to the surgeon.

sell
2 · Pricing

Flat per-encounter. Fair market value. No revenue share.

Clear

Pricing is published on the home page: a monthly subscription at a fixed dollar amount per seat (Free, Core $199/mo, Pro $299/mo) plus a flat $20 per encounter processed through the platform. The same fees apply to every surgeon on the platform regardless of specialty mix, payer mix, referral volume, or downstream revenue.

The platform fee is not a percentage of collections, a percentage of denials recovered, a per-referral fee, or a bounty tied to any downstream service. This structure is the same flat per-encounter framework the OIG has recognized in Advisory Opinions (including OIG AO 25-03) as consistent with the Anti-Kickback Statute safe-harbor principles for personal services and management contracts under 42 CFR § 1001.952(d).

Fair-market-value benchmarking is performed annually against third-party healthcare software pricing data. The FMV analysis is maintained internally and is available to engaged counsel on request.

3 · Referrals & downstream services

We do not pay for referrals. We do not take a cut.

Clear

SurgeonValue does not compensate any person or entity for referring a patient, ordering a service, or selecting a device, implant, or facility. The platform's resource directory (used to help patients find ancillary services such as home care, imaging, or second opinions) is a non-paid directory — listings are not sold, sponsored, or weighted by payment.

Co-op.care, Altru.care, and ClinicalSwipe are separately branded sibling products within the SolvingHealth ecosystem. A surgeon's patient may be presented with those options through the resource directory, but SurgeonValue receives no compensation from those entities for any patient who selects them, and the surgeon receives no compensation for presenting the options. Patient choice is preserved at every step.

4 · Surgeon autonomy

The surgeon signs. SurgeonValue assists.

Clear

Every billable code, prior authorization letter, clinical summary, or coding recommendation surfaced by the platform is a suggestion. The surgeon retains final authority to accept, edit, or reject every suggestion before it is submitted to a payer or entered into the EHR. No code is submitted from SurgeonValue to a clearinghouse or payer without surgeon approval.

This is reinforced by the ClinicalSwipe attestation layer: every AI-generated artifact carries a physician review signature before release. The architecture implements the AI-governance principle endorsed in AMA clinical AI guidance and aligns with the physician-in-the-loop framing central to the Lantos et al. JAMA 2026 editorial on clinical AI.

5 · HIPAA & data processing

Business Associate. BAA with every practice.

Clear

SurgeonValue is a HIPAA Business Associate to each surgical practice that licenses the platform. A BAA is executed before any PHI is transmitted. Sub-processors (model vendors, cloud infrastructure, clearinghouses, EHR integrators) are each covered by sub-BAAs with minimum-necessary data-flow restrictions.

Patient PHI is processed only for the permitted purposes under the BAA: supporting the surgeon's treatment, payment, and healthcare-operations activities. PHI is never used to train foundation models and is never shared with any entity outside the BAA chain. Model inference is routed exclusively to BAA-executed endpoints.

integration_instructions
6 · EHR & clearinghouse integration

Sanctioned APIs. No scraping. No backchannels.

Clear

SurgeonValue integrates with practice EHRs (Epic, Cerner, athenahealth, eClinicalWorks, ModMed, NextGen, AdvancedMD and others) exclusively through each EHR's sanctioned developer program — SMART on FHIR for API access, formal integration agreements for read/write workflows, and in every case at the direction and authorization of the practice that owns the patient data.

Claims submission, eligibility checks, and denials workflows flow through licensed clearinghouses (Waystar, Availity, Change Healthcare, Office Ally) under executed connectivity agreements. SurgeonValue does not originate claims — the surgeon's billing entity originates every claim under its own NPI and tax ID.

7 · CJR-X episode-based care

We help surgeons track episodes. We do not take a cut of shared savings.

Yellow

SurgeonValue surfaces CJR-X (Comprehensive Joint Replacement - Expanded) episode analytics, cost-of-care benchmarks, and outcome tracking. The platform's fee for these features remains a flat subscription — it is not a percentage of any shared savings or gainsharing payment the practice may receive under the CJR-X model.

Open item: If a practice that uses SurgeonValue also participates in a gainsharing arrangement with a convening entity, that separate arrangement is governed by its own OIG gainsharing framework and is not a SurgeonValue transaction. We are completing an advisory opinion request to confirm this separation remains clean under the CJR-X final rule. Flagged YELLOW until on file.

8 · Entity separation

SurgeonValue is software. The surgeon is the provider.

SurgeonValue Inc. is a Delaware C corporation that licenses software. Every surgeon who uses the platform does so through their own professional corporation, medical group, or employment arrangement. SurgeonValue has no ownership interest in any clinical entity and no clinical entity has ownership in SurgeonValue beyond arm's-length equity held by founders, employees, and investors.

The SolvingHealth ecosystem includes Altru.care (physician-owned DPC practice) and co-op.care (worker-owned care cooperative). Those entities do not share ownership with SurgeonValue's clinical customers. Referral paths between ecosystem entities follow the non-paid resource-directory model described in Section 3.
